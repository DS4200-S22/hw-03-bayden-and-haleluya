HTML+CSS & Marks+Channels


The bubble marker is an attribute, the % of the pie is an attribute, and the positions on the axis are also attributes because they can all be specific properties that can be measured or logged. The marks in this chart is the areas of the pie charts. These marks are being used as items/nodes. The channels in this channels are both the vertical and horizontal positions of the pie charts.

In this bump chart, the individual points are items and the line between two points are links. The marks in this chart are points and lines. These marks are being used as link, specifically as connections. The channels in this channels are the colors of the points and the tilt of the lines.


The mind map uses the items, attributes, and links data types. Each node is an item, repesenting an object/topic. Each connection between nodes is a link, which show how the relationships work. Colors could also be used to represent different attributes. Mind maps use points as marks, to show each item. The use lines to show links. For channels, colors would be used to show any attributes.


The stacked bar chart is a visualization which utilizes items and attributes to represent data. The items can be represented by lines for marks, which would just be each bar. The attributes are represented by color and the position vertically. The color would be the division within each bar. The position is how far up it goes on the x-axis.
